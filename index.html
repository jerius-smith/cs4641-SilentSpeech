Introduction

Silent Speller aims to recognize language from silent utterances. This technology is useful for people with speech impairments, in noisy environments, and in social situations that restrict speech. Researchers have explored using different sensors, like surface electromyography and video, to capture data that encodes speech. We further existing research on speech recognition by using Hidden Markov Models to recognize silent utterances at the letter level, and increase the accuracy of our recognizer by introducing a context training and a statistical grammar. Unlike other devices, SmartPalate is partially invisible and can be used when walking. Our goals is to link sensor information to letters.

System Overview

The SmartPalate device consists of 124 capacitive sensors and each sensor reads as a 1 or a 0 at every time step. There are orginally 100 time steps for each sample but we reduced each sample down to just 15.
